BIOGRAPHY

Fyodor Dostoyevsky (1821 - 1881)

Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoevsky is one of the most important Russian novelists of the 19th century. In his works, he delved into the depths of the human soul and conveyed the inner conflicts of the individual, moral and philosophical problems in a literary language. Dostoevsky, who started his writing career at a young age, led a difficult life. His years of exile in Siberia and his experiences there left deep traces in his works.

His works include novels such as ‘Crime and Punishment’, ‘The Idiot’, ‘The Brothers Karamazov’ and ‘Notes from Underground’, which are considered masterpieces of world literature. In these works, he deals with deep philosophical themes such as moral questions, the complex psychological structure of man and the belief in God. Dostoevsky is considered to be one of the writers who best describes the complexity of the human soul in the world of literature.
